Why Some Usernames Are Protected

You may have noticed that certain usernames on our platform are marked as protected. This is intentional, and it’s all about keeping the community safe, fair, and respectful.

1. Preventing Impersonation

Protected usernames ensure that no one can pretend to be an admin, moderator, or other trusted role. This reduces the risk of confusion, scams, or abuse.

Example: A username like “Admin” or “Moderator” is reserved so regular users can’t take it.

Apart from just those specific names, some admins and staff members have their usernames protected too, to prevent impersonation.

2. Respect for Admins & Staff

Admins and staff members work hard to support the community. Reserving certain usernames shows respect for their roles and avoids diluting their identity.

3. A Clearer Community Experience

When usernames are unique and authentic, it’s easier to trust who you’re talking to. This helps maintain a positive atmosphere where everyone knows who’s who.

Note: Admins will also have an admin badge next to their username, so you can easily identify them.

How It Works

Our system automatically blocks attempts to register usernames that are too similar to protected ones. This includes case differences, added numbers, or small misspellings.

If you accidentally run into this restriction, don’t worry—just try a different username that reflects you personally.
